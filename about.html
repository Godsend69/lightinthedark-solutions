My Story

The Man Who Refused to Disappear

My real name is Spiridon Tsakonas. I was born in Greece — a country that has known both the heights of civilization and the depths of collapse. I have watched people I love lose everything: their livelihoods, their dignity, their sense of self. And I have faced the same losses in my own life.

I am a father. I am a husband. I am a man who has stood in darkness so complete that light seemed not merely distant, but fictional. And I am someone who discovered — through years of struggle, study, and sheer refusal to surrender — that darkness is never the final word.

I write under the name Cyrus Nash — a pen name chosen deliberately. Cyrus, the ancient king who freed the captive. Nash, from the old word for "ash" — what remains after the fire. What survives. What endures.

I created Light in the Dark Solutions in 2016 because I had accumulated enough experience — both personal and in working with others — to know that I could be genuinely useful to people at the exact moments when they need it most.

This is not a hobby. This is not a brand. This is a mission. Every person who comes through this door carries something irreplaceable — and my single purpose is to help them find it again.

The Foundation

A Philosophy of Light

Everything I do is grounded in three principles that I have tested not only intellectually, but in the furnace of real life.

I

Sovereignty

The human being is not a resource. Not a variable. Not a consumer unit in someone else's model. Every soul deserves — and has the capacity for — the conditions of self-authorship. My work begins and ends with this conviction.

II

Illumination

Truth does not shout. It glows. My work is to locate the places where a person has been denied clarity — about themselves, their history, their options — and to carry honest light there without distortion, without agenda, without fear.

III

Will

Talent is a gift. Will is a choice — made in the dark, without guarantees, against opposition. The greatest thing I can offer another person is the rediscovery of their own capacity to choose. Everything else follows from that single, radical act.

The Humanitarian Vision

The L.I.F.E. Project

The L.I.F.E. Project is not a program. It is a direction. A north star that has guided every piece of work I have ever done — long before I had a name for it.

Liberation. Illumination. Freedom. Empowerment. These are not abstract values. They are specific outcomes that I believe every human being has the right to experience — not as luxury, but as minimum condition for a life worth living.

The project has two dimensions. The first is individual: the one-on-one work I do with people in crisis, in transition, in search of meaning. The second is collective: the writing, the essays, and eventually the wider initiatives that carry this work beyond individual sessions and into the broader conversation humanity is having — and failing to resolve — about what it means to truly be free.

The Age of Obedience — the book I am writing — is the intellectual and philosophical backbone of the L.I.F.E. Project. It is my attempt to give the world a map of how we got here, and a compass for how to leave.
